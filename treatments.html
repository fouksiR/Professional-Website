Fertility Assessment and Counselling

Your first consultation is a comprehensive, personalised review focused on understanding your fertility goals, medical background, and any challenges you may be facing. During this appointment, we will explore your reproductive history, menstrual patterns, lifestyle factors, and any previous attempts to conceive.

We will discuss key aspects of your fertility health, including hormone function and cycle regularity, and identify any factors that may be influencing your ability to conceive. Based on this discussion, I will recommend appropriate investigations, which may include blood tests, specialised pelvic ultrasound, and semen analysis.

Once your results are available, we will meet again to go through them in detail. I will explain what they mean, outline potential treatment pathways, discuss expected timelines and success rates, and provide supportive counselling to help you make informed decisions.

Together, we will develop a tailored fertility plan designed to support you at every stage of your journey toward pregnancy.

Natural Cycle Tracking

Monitoring the natural menstrual cycle is a helpful option for people trying to conceive without medical intervention. Cycle tracking allows us to identify when ovulation is occurring and define the most fertile window, helping guide the best timing for intercourse.

This can be achieved using a combination of blood tests and targeted ultrasound scans performed in my consulting rooms. Ongoing tracking gives individuals and couples greater insight into their cycles and supports a proactive, informed approach to optimising natural conception.

Ovulation Induction

Ovulation induction is a treatment used to encourage the ovaries to produce and release an egg, particularly for people who ovulate irregularly or not at all. It usually involves short courses of medication taken early in the cycle, commonly over five days, such as letrozole.

Response to treatment is closely monitored with ultrasound and blood tests. This allows us to assess follicle development and accurately time ovulation, maximising the chances of pregnancy. Ovulation induction provides a structured and targeted way to support natural conception by promoting predictable and healthy ovulation.

Intrauterine Insemination (IUI)

Intrauterine insemination is a fertility procedure in which prepared sperm is placed directly into the uterus around the time of ovulation. This short, minimally invasive procedure bypasses the cervix and positions the sperm closer to the egg, increasing the likelihood of fertilisation.

IUI is often combined with ovulation induction to optimise timing and improve success rates. It is a relatively simple treatment option that can enhance the chances of conception for selected individuals and couples.

Fertility Preservation

Egg Freezing

Egg freezing, also known as oocyte cryopreservation, involves collecting eggs from the ovaries, freezing them, and storing them for potential use in the future. This allows individuals to preserve fertility and maintain the option of pregnancy later in life.

Sperm Freezing

Sperm freezing (sperm cryopreservation) is a method of preserving sperm for future use. It is often chosen by individuals who are about to undergo medical treatments that could affect fertility, such as chemotherapy, or by those who wish to defer parenthood.

Frozen sperm can later be thawed and used in fertility treatments including IUI or IVF. This approach provides flexibility and reassurance, allowing people to plan future family building in a way that suits their personal circumstances.

Assisted Reproductive Technology

IVF (with ICSI when required)

In vitro fertilisation (IVF) is a treatment in which eggs and sperm are combined in the laboratory to create embryos. A selected embryo is then transferred into the uterus with the aim of achieving pregnancy.

Pregnancy & Women's Health

Early Pregnancy Scanning and Advice

Early pregnancy ultrasound is usually performed between six and eight weeks of gestation. This scan confirms that the pregnancy is developing in the uterus and assesses for a fetal heartbeat.

The appointment also provides an opportunity to discuss early pregnancy care, including lifestyle guidance, nutrition, activity, and what to expect in the coming weeks, helping establish a strong and reassuring start to pregnancy.

PCOS Management

Management of polycystic ovary syndrome outside of fertility care focuses on long-term health, symptom control, and prevention of complications. Treatment aims to improve issues such as irregular cycles, acne, weight changes, and insulin resistance.

Lifestyle strategies, including nutrition and regular physical activity, play an important role in supporting metabolic and hormonal health. Medications such as the oral contraceptive pill may be used to regulate periods and manage androgen-related symptoms, while metformin can help improve insulin sensitivity.

Ongoing monitoring and early intervention can reduce the risk of longer-term health concerns, including type 2 diabetes and cardiovascular disease, and support overall wellbeing.

Reproductive Assessment Before Endometriosis Surgery

For individuals diagnosed with endometriosis who are planning surgery, a detailed reproductive assessment before the operation is an important part of care. Endometriosis and pelvic surgery can both affect ovarian reserve and future fertility, so understanding your baseline reproductive health allows for informed, forward-planning decisions.

This assessment includes a thorough review of fertility goals, menstrual history, prior treatments, and pelvic imaging. Investigations may involve hormone testing (including ovarian reserve markers), specialist ultrasound, and, where relevant, semen analysis for a partner.

The aim is to clarify current fertility potential, discuss how surgery may influence reproductive outcomes, and explore options such as timing of conception, fertility preservation, or assisted reproductive treatments. This ensures that surgical management is aligned with both symptom control and long-term reproductive plans.

Male Fertility & Endocrinology

Male Infertility

Male factors contribute to fertility challenges in a significant proportion of couples. A comprehensive male fertility evaluation focuses on identifying hormonal, genetic, anatomical, lifestyle, and environmental contributors to impaired sperm production or function.

Assessment typically includes a detailed medical and reproductive history, semen analysis, hormonal testing, and further investigations where indicated. Management may involve lifestyle optimisation, medical treatment, referral for surgical management, fertility preservation, or the use of assisted reproductive technologies such as IUI or IVF/ICSI.

Care is individualised, evidence-based, and integrated with partner assessment to ensure that treatment strategies are targeted, efficient, and appropriate.

Menopause

Menopause care focuses on supporting individuals through the hormonal transition of perimenopause and menopause, addressing both symptoms and long-term health.

Common concerns include changes in menstrual patterns, hot flushes, sleep disturbance, mood changes, cognitive symptoms, vaginal and urinary symptoms, sexual health concerns, bone health, and cardiovascular risk. Assessment involves a detailed symptom review, medical history, and where appropriate, targeted investigations.

Management is personalised and may include lifestyle strategies, hormonal and non-hormonal treatments, and preventive health planning. The goal is to relieve symptoms, improve quality of life, and reduce long-term health risks through informed, evidence-based care.

Complex Reproductive Endocrinology

Complex reproductive endocrinology care is designed for individuals with challenging or uncommon hormonal and reproductive conditions. This includes disorders of puberty, menstrual dysfunction, premature ovarian insufficiency, resistant PCOS, hypothalamic amenorrhoea, endocrine causes of infertility, complex menopausal presentations, and fertility preservation in medical or surgical conditions.

These consultations involve detailed endocrine evaluation, advanced hormonal testing, careful interpretation of imaging and laboratory results, and integrated long-term planning. Management may require coordinated care with other specialists and a combination of medical, reproductive, and preventive strategies.

The focus is on delivering precise, individualised care for complex presentations, supporting both current health needs and future reproductive and hormonal wellbeing.
